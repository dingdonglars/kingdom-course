# Module 5.1 starter — Roblox Studio tour

This module has no real code. The artefact is your `journal/5.1-studio-tour.md` notes from clicking around Studio for the first time.

## What's in here

- `journal/5.1-studio-tour.md` — a journal template for the module. Open it as you click around Studio and jot anything that surprises you, anything that confuses you, and the names of the four panels in your own words.
- `roblox-kingdom/` — a placeholder folder for your Roblox project. Real Roblox files have the extension `.rbxl` and live on your local install — they're large binaries that don't go into git happily. Keep this folder around as a marker; treat the actual `.rbxl` like a Word document on your machine.

## How to use it

1. Open Studio first, before opening this folder. The point of the module is the click-around tour.
2. Once you've had a look at the four panels and run the one-line `print("Hello world!")` script, come back here and write three or four sentences in `journal/5.1-studio-tour.md` about what you saw.
3. The notes don't need to be polished. They're for you to look back at in two weeks when you're elbow-deep in RemoteEvents and want to remember which folder is which.

## Common gotcha

If you save the place inside Studio (`File → Save to File`), the resulting `.rbxl` is large and binary. Don't `git add` those — keep them on your machine. When you reach M5.1's "publish" cousin (M5.8), the place will live on Roblox's servers under a URL, not in this folder.
