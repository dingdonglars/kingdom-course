# Block 7 — Roblox Kingdom *(Bonus)*

> **Your game lives where your friends already play.**

**What you'll have at the end:** A version of your Kingdom that your friends play **on Roblox**. The engine — the one you've now ported four times across console, file, API, and browser — survives one more port: from C# to Luau. The meta-lesson lands across language boundaries.

**Estimated effort:** 10 weeks at 4–6 hrs/week  ·  **Wraps:** M6 *Kingdom v5 — Roblox-Published*

---

## Why this block?

This block exists for one reason: **so the meta-lesson lands hardest.** The same engine, ported to a different language entirely, in a different runtime entirely. After Block 7, you have *lived* the lesson "the model is forever; the runtime is a detail."

It's also the block that lets your friends actually play your game where they already are. Roblox is where the audience is.

This is a bonus block — there's no penalty for stopping at M5. But if you do this one, you finish the course.

## What you'll learn (named)

- Roblox Studio — the IDE, parts, models, scripts
- Luau — Lua with types; the syntax bridge from C# / JS
- OOP in Luau — tables-as-classes, metatables
- Roblox-specific concepts — Workspace, RemoteEvents, ServerScriptService, client/server
- Porting an engine across languages
- The visual world — modeling in Studio
- Roblox DataStore — persistence, Roblox-style
- Publishing — your friends play your game

## What you'll build (the climb)

- **Week 43:** Roblox Studio tour.
- **Week 44:** Luau basics.
- **Weeks 45–46:** OOP in Luau, Roblox-specific concepts.
- **Weeks 47–48:** port the engine — buildings, resources, events, game loop.
- **Weeks 49–50:** the visual world — tiles, models, click-to-buy.
- **Week 51:** Roblox DataStore.
- **Week 52:** Publish. Capstone reflection.

## Brag-worthy outcome

A public Roblox game your friends play *on Roblox*. With your engine. Written by you.
