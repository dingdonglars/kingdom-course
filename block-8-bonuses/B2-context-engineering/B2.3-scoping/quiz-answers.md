# Quiz answers — B2.3

## 1. a
Scoping = per-task framing. Goal in one sentence; what's in/out; conventions to follow; traps to avoid; success criteria. The opposite of vague prompts.

## 2. a
Three sentences cover small asks: in `<file>`, add `<signature>` that does `<one sentence>`. Match style of `<example>`. Don't `<known trap>`. Use the full template only when the ask is larger.

## 3. a
The AI fills in your blanks. If you don't say "don't refactor surrounding code," it might. If you don't say "don't add validation," it might. Non-goals are the cheapest way to prevent over-eager output.

## 4. a
Success criteria turn the prompt into a checklist. The AI can self-check; you can verify in seconds. Without them, "done" is fuzzy — both for the AI generating and for you reviewing.

## 5. a
Treat the prompt like a contract: clear goal, explicit limits, defined deliverable. The AI executes against the contract. A vague contract is the *cause* of vague output, not a flaw in the AI.
