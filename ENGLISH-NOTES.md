# English in This Course — Notes for You

> Why the course is in English, what scaffolding exists, what moves are permitted, and what stays English no matter what.

## Why English

The course is in English because **your English is comfortable**, and reading a year of code, lessons, and developer documentation in English is one of the most valuable secondary skills you'll come out with. It's not a test; it's an exposure dose.

We don't drill English at you. We just keep everything in English so you grow into it naturally over twelve months — without anyone calling that growth *learning English*.

## What we do to make it easier

- **Plain language standard.** Lessons target approximately CEFR B1–B2 — short sentences, present tense, second person, no academic register. We deliberately keep the language simple so the *concepts* are the hard part, not the *words*.
- **"Words to watch" sidebars.** Every lesson that introduces new terms has a short sidebar at the top with one-line definitions.
- **Pronunciation footnotes** for unguessable terms (*OAuth (oh-auth)*, *Kubernetes (koo-ber-net-eez)*).
- **Idiom callouts** when an idiom matters (*rubber duck debugging*, *yak shaving*) — explained inline.
- **Living glossary** at `GLOSSARY.md` — every term ever introduced, alphabetised, with the lesson where it first appears.

## What you can do — the permitted moves

- **Ask Claude *"what does this English word mean?"*** — anywhere, any time. **This never counts against the 20-minute rule** and you don't need to show what you tried. Reaching for a definition is normal, not stuck.
- **Ask Claude in Portuguese** if the question is genuinely about language understanding. Claude is allowed to answer in Portuguese when explicitly asked. (You won't need this often, but it's there.)
- **Translate-and-restate.** If a paragraph isn't landing, paste it into a translator, read the Portuguese version, then write back **in English** what you understood. The restatement is the learning moment. This is *encouraged*.
- **Ask Lars in person** if a concept truly isn't crossing the language barrier. Lars will explain in English (slower, simpler), and you'll restate it back in English together.

## What stays English no matter what

- **Code, identifiers, comments.**
- **Commit messages.**
- **PR titles and bodies.**
- **README files in your repo.**
- **Your `journal/wins.md`** entries.
- **Your own notes** if you keep them in your repo.

These are the things a future employer or collaborator sees. Producing English is non-negotiable. *Receiving* English has a lot of scaffolding. *Producing* English stays English-only.

## When in doubt

If you're stuck on language and the scaffolding above isn't working: post in `#help` with the paragraph that broke and what specifically isn't landing. We adjust the lesson if it's a course problem; we work it through together if it's a learning moment.
